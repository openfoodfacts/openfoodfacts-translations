If a picture is worth 1000 words, it is also worth 1000 digits ! You can now easily create charts to visualize the data of your choice for the products of your choice from those we collected together on Open Food Facts.

In practice, I completed the advanced search form (which on the website is now titled Advanced Search and Charts , link at the top right) so you can choose to view the products matching your search criteria (name, categories, labels, brands, and nutritional composition etc.) in graphical form.

제품은 선택한 2 개의 축에 배치됩니다. For now it can be any nutrients or the number of additives.

Here's what it can do if you choose to represent the Sodas that were collected during the Sodas Operation according to their sugar content and the number of additives they contain :



그래프를 만들면, 그래프에 링크가 생길겁니다 그 링크는 당신이 그래프를 sns나, 이메일 등을 통해 공유할 수 있게 합니다.

The graph on the site is dynamic: by hovering over each point with the mouse, you can see the corresponding product, with a link to the complete product sheet.

Depending on the products you represent, you can choose the most relevant axes, for example the salt and fat content for deli meats, carbohydrates and fats for breakfast cereals,…

Even more graphing possibilities

 This "Cloud of Points" graph is the first type of graph that can be created directly on Open Food Facts. Of course, you can create many more by downloading data and analyzing it with software like Open Office, Google Refine or Excel. But it seems very interesting to me to easily create graphics directly on Open Food Facts: 2 steps in less (the download of data and their import into an external software) make the creation of graphics much more accessible to the general public.

Developing graphing possibilities on Open Food Facts has another advantage: we can imagine features specifically adapted to food products. For example, I would like to add a graph to visualize the dyes used in products as a "pie chart", with the color of each slice calculated for each combination of dyes. For cheeses whose beautiful crust is colored with carmines (red) and annatto (yellow), we would have a nice orange slice.
