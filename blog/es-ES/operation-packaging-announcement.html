Open Food Facts is launching a new project, hand in hand with the ADEME (Ecological Transition Agency), to improve the collection of packaging data and thus better understand the issue of reduction of the environmental impact of packaging. 

The aim of this project is to allow ADEME teams (and anyone interested in the subject) to analyze the data collected on packaging: shapes, materials, weight, recyclability…. This data will help support eco-design, the reduction of over-packaging and the deployment of environmental labeling on food products.

¿Por dónde empezar? ¡Por yogurs!

Food packaging can be scary, there are so many of them! That's why we decided to tackle one product category in particular, yogurts.

¿Por qué los yogures?

Porque se venden 15 mil millones de potes de yogur de poliestireno al año en Francia aproximadamente (es decir, aprox. 250 potes de yogur / año / Francia) 😨😨😨

  • only 2% is recycled (in factories in Spain and Germany). El resto se quema o termina en la naturaleza 😓
  • Cada segundo = 500 envases de yogur son arrojados en Francia

(source: article "Plastic pollution: why France remains addicted to its 15  billion-polystyrene yogurt pots polystyrene" - Le Monde).

In 2014 Open Food Facts launched the collection operation "What's IN my yogurt? ". It's high time to find out "What's AROUND my yogurt" now!

Se buscan: comedores de yogur!

¡Así que estamos haciendo un llamamiento a los consumidores de yogur! It can be any type of yoghurt (milk or vegetable (example)). 

Cómo ingresar datos: paso a paso

Once you have finished tasting your yoghurt, here are the steps to follow to enter the valuable data on its packaging: 

1. Wash / dry all packaging components (e.g. jar, lid, etc.)

2. Sign in or create an account on Open Food Facts (site or app) - it's free, your personal data is kept completely private and there is no there are no ads!

Aplicación móvil

a. On Open Food Facts (site or mobile), find the product in question (either by scanning it or by looking for its barcode number in the search bar). 

b. Click on MODIFY THE SHEET product. 

3. In the PACKAGING DATA section, enter the data concerning:

the number of units (e.g. if the yoghurt pack is 4 pots),

la forma,

el material (generalmente se encuentra grabado en la parte inferior de la olla),

instrucciones de reciclaje (a veces en el cartón del empaque),

el peso (si tiene una báscula con precisión de 0,00 gramos; de ahí la importancia de asegurarse de que el embalaje esté limpio),

y la cantidad del producto contenido por unidad. 

ej. I have a pack of 4 yoghurt pots (surrounded by cardboard). 

Once the packaging is empty / clean / dry, I will fill in the information and weigh one by one each of the elements (for the quantities, I will mark: x4 jars, x4 lids, x1 box ). 

If you have all the information, check the box “All parts of the packaging are listed”.

What if I don't know something (e.g. weight)? 😳

I enter the data I have and I DO NOT check the box “All parts of the packaging are listed".
