The “Packaging” operation – ADEME x Open Food Facts

Open Food Facts is launching a new project, hand in hand with the French Ecological Transition Agency (ADEME), to improve the collection of packaging data and thus better understand the issue of reducing the environmental impact of packaging.

The purpose of this project is to allow ADEME teams (and anyone interested in the subject) to analyze the data collected on packaging: shapes, materials, weight, recyclability, etc. This data will help support eco-design, the reduction of over-packaging and the deployment of environmental labeling on food products.

Where to start ? By yogurts!

Food packaging can be scary, there are so many! This is why we decided to tackle one product category in particular, yogurts.

Why yogurts?

Because15 billion polystyrene yoghurt pots are sold each year in France approximately (i.e. approximately 250 pots of yogurt / year / French)

  • only 2% is recycled (in factories in Spain and Germany). The rest cremated or ends up in the wild
  • Every second = 500 yoghurt pots thrown away in France

(source: article “Plastic pollution: why France remains addicted to its 15 billion polystyrene yoghurt pots” – Le Monde).

In 2014 Open Food Facts launched the collection operation"What's IN my yogurt? ». It's high time to know "What's AROUND my yogurt" now!

WANTED: YOGURT EATERS!

We are therefore appealing to yogurt eaters! It can be all types of yogurts (with milk or vegetables (example)).

How to enter data: step by step

Once you have finished tasting your yogurt, here are the steps to follow to enter the valuable data on its packaging:

1. Wash / dry all packaging components (e.g. jar, lid, etc.)

2. Log in or create an account on Open Food Facts(site or app) – it’s free, your personal data remains completely private and there are no ads!

Site Open Food Facts

Appli mobile

a. On Open Food Facts (site or mobile), find the product in question (either by scanning it or by searching for its barcode number in the search bar).

b. Click on MODIFY THE SHEET product.

3. In the section PACKAGING DATA, enter the data concerning:

the number of units (e.g. if the yoghurt pack is 4 pots),

the form,

the material (usually found in relief on the underside of the pot),

recycling instructions (sometimes on the cardboard of the pack),

the weight (if you have an accurate scale at 0,00 grams; hence the importance of ensuring that the packaging is clean),

and the amount of product contained per unit.

ex. I have a pack of 4 pots of yogurt (surrounded by cardboard).

Once the packages are empty/clean/dry, I will fill in the information and weigh one by one each of the elements (for quantities, I will mark: x4 pots, x4 opercules, x1 carton). 

If you have all the information, check the box “All parts of the packaging are listed”.

What if I don't know something (eg weight)?

I enter the data I have and I DO NOT tick the box “All parts of the package are listed”.

You can also refresh images.

And of course,“Save” your good work !
