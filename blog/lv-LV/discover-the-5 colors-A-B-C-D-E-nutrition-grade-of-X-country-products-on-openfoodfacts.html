Enerģijas vērtējums ir svarīga izvēle, pērkot ledusskapi. Uzturvielu rādītājs drīz var būt arī pārtikai.



Uzturvērtības marķējuma vienkāršošana uz iesaiņojuma, lai palīdzētu patērētājiem izvēlēties veselīgāku pārtiku, ir piecu krāsu vērtēšanas sistēmas mērķis, ko ierosināja profesors Sergejs Hercbergs no Francijas Nacionālās uztura un veselības programmas.



Tādā pašā garā kā ierīces enerģijas marķējums, šī sistēma nodrošina vērtējumu no A līdz E (katrs no tiem ir saistīts ar zaļu līdz sarkanu krāsu), kas brīvprātīgi tiktu parādīts izstrādājumu priekšpusē. . Šī sistēma ir daļa no Marisol Touraine veselības likumprojekta, kas tiks apspriests Nacionālajā asamblejā 2015. gada 31. martā.


More fruits and vegetables, protein and fiber, fewer calories, sugar, saturated fat and salt.

"5 fruits and vegetables a day" , "Less fat, less sweet, less salty" : the notes of color try to synthesize these messages well known to the general public but not necessarily easy to apply when shopping .

In order to establish a food score, a nutritional score is first calculated that takes into account the content of elements found to be nutritionally favorable (fruits and vegetables, protein and fiber) and elements considered to be unfavorable. energy, simple sugars, saturated fatty acids, and sodium corresponding to salt). The green, yellow, orange, fuchsia and red color notes (A, B, C, D, E) correspond to different intervals of the nutritional score.

A note to compare foods when shopping

Unlike other rating systems (such as recommended daily intake percentages), the score is independent of the portion indicated by the manufacturer, which is often different for each brand and is sometimes underestimated compared to the portion actually consumed.
Not linking the note to the size of the portion makes it possible to compare the notes of different products before choosing which one to buy.

Color notes on Open Food Facts

We contacted Professor Hercberg at the end of September 2014 to ask him for the exact formula of the nutritional score and the thresholds for determining the 5-color notes A / B / C / D / E recommended in his report "Proposals for a new impetus of the policy public health nutrition as part of the National Health Strategy " .

Professor Hercberg's team sent us the calculation formula and all the information needed to calculate the score and the nutritional score, so we were able to apply the formula to the products in the Open Food Facts collaborative database. Scoring is possible for products for which a sufficiently complete nutrition table is shown on the packaging. To date, we have been able to determine the rating of more than 10,000 food products.

View nutrition notes on your smartphone in augmented reality

We display nutritional score and color ratings on each Open Food Facts product sheet, and are also featured in our Android , iPhone and Windows Phone mobile apps .

While waiting for the possible adoption of the color note system, and its possible voluntary application by the manufacturers on the products, you can now scan the product barcodes with the Open Food Facts application to see the nutritional ratings.

On Android mobiles (and soon iPhone), you do not even need to scan the barcode: thanks to the visual recognition technology of the company Moodstocks , simply place your phone in front of a product to see its nutritional score , and that even without internet connection, which is very useful especially in stores.

Enthusiastic consumers, more critical manufacturers

The nutritional score and its implementation on Open Food Facts were presented on Friday, February 27th in the show In search of news: "Sugars, fat, why are we all addicted?" on the D8 channel.



In the minutes and hours following the broadcast of the report, Open Food Facts had its biggest peak of registrations and received many positive messages by e-mail and on our Facebook group . It is an enthusiasm that we have seen every time we present the Open Food Facts project to the general public and the implementation of the nutritional notes on the mobile application.

This enthusiasm is however far from being shared by representatives of the food industry such as the National Association of the Food Industry (ANIA) for whom the system of color notes is simplistic, stigmatizing products and making them feel guilty about them. consumers.

The weight of words, the shock of data

Beyond the word battles, the Open Food Facts association aims at transparency and openness of data on food products. We are convinced that these data are data of public interest - they are too important to be kept closed - and that is why we started 3 years ago to build a collaborative, free and open database , in which more than 1,500 contributors added data from more than 35,000 products.

These data have very numerous applications: decryption of labels (additives, allergens, etc.), comparison and research of products, education ... a dozen reuses of Open Food Facts data have already been inventoried.

This data can test, evaluate and refine a nutritional scoring system as a whole by applying it to 10,000 products rather than to weaker samples or focusing on emblematic but sometimes anecdotal examples.

To this end, we have put in place a series of tools so that everyone can test the system of color notes of the National Nutrition and Health Program:

Display of the distribution of nutritional notes

The Open Food Facts website allows you to explore the database in different facets such as product categories, brands, labels, and now nutritional notes. It is now very easy to obtain the distribution of nutritional notes for all products or for a given brand or category, for example breakfast cereals, and to display the corresponding products of a certain grade.


Nutritional notes of breakfast cereals - Source Open Food Facts

Nutrition notes by product category

It is interesting to compare the nutritional notes of the different categories of products. We can see the categories of the best rated products (fruits and vegetables, compotes) and the lowest rated (chocolate, sausage, mayonnaise).

You can also see which categories have products that have a wide variety of ratings. It is for these categories that the rating system is the most useful because it allows consumers to take into account the nutritional score in their choices, and it can encourage manufacturers to improve the formulation of their products for them get better grades.

On the other hand, the score is less useful for categories where all products have almost the same nutritional values as honeys that all obtain the D score. The system can then compare to similar categories such as spreads pasta or jams or honey .

Nutritional notes of color in interactive charts

With a few clicks, the Open Food Facts search form allows you to create interactive charts that show all the products corresponding to certain criteria (for example category, brand, label ...) on 2 axes that you can choose (all nutrients, minerals, vitamins, number of additives, nutritional score etc.).
