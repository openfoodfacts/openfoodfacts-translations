Nossa experiência no Capitole du Libre

Depoimento de Gabriel, que apresentou Open Food Facts, durante o Capitole du Libre

“Evento líder no domínio do software livre, o Capitole du Libre 2022 realizado nos dias 19 e 20 de novembro em Toulouse foi um grande sucesso. Foi um evento realmente ótimo em muitos aspectos: 

Nós tivemos a oportunidade de conhecer as comunidades francesas de código aberto, conteúdo aberto e dados abertos, de manter um estande apresentando todas as atividades mais recentes no Open Food Facts e de dar palestras.

Localizado entre o OpenStreetMap e o OSGeo, é onde você encontraria o estande do Open Food Facts. Durante todo o fim de semana, muitos visitantes passaram pelo nosso estande, chegando a um total de cerca de cem pessoas ao final do evento. 

Num evento deste tipo, o público é sempre muito simpático, motivado para saber mais sobre como poderá potencialmente contribuir para o projeto. Preparamos vários cartazes, embalagens de produtos e QR Codes, com objetivo de testar nossa aplicação, para participar na campanha de doações ou para preencher um formulário para entrar na comunidade contributiva do Open Food Facts (pode ler mais sobre o Contributor Skill Pool neste artigo). 

Every discussion we had was enjoyable and allowed to both identify the diverse profiles of the people interested in Open Food Facts and to make them discover the project, thus spreading once more the word about our open project.

The Capitole du Libre allowed me to meet various other Open Source projects. Discussing with these many committed people who share their work and knowledge is always a fulfilling experience.

I was then given an opportunity to share my knowledge about Open Food Facts to around thirty people during a talk I gave on the theme “Artificial Intelligence for food transparency at Open Food Facts” (FR version). This conference was addressed to everyone interested by the project or AI in general, with or without technical background. After presenting the history of Open Food Facts, its objectives and its community, I presented on the way Robotoff works and its use of Machine Learning to improve the quality of the database.

I was asked several different questions after my talk, indicating to me that the audience had been interested by what they had heard.

In a nutshell, I was extremely pleased to participate in the Capitole du Libre 2022 for Open Food Facts !"

Por que apresentar o Open Food Facts? 🤔

Open Food Facts relies on citizen crowdsourcing of data

25 000 contributors have added 2.6 million products to Open Food Facts. O banco de dados inclui produtos de mais de 100 países diferentes Mas a maior parte deles vêm da França - onde o Open Food Facts foi lançado em 2012 - e de alguns países como Espanha, Reino Unido, EUA e Bélgica, onde temos alguns contribuintes muito motivados. We would like this motivation to spread: we need to add products from your country and try to reach a critical mass of products. More importantly, the project needs to create and develop strong local communities of contributors.

Criar comunidades locais

To create this community, we need people who can present Open Food Facts in front of as many different audiences as possible, from the most obvious to the most unlikely ones. In France, we have accepted all invitations to present our project, and each time we have quickly connected with the people who attended our presentations. Food is a universal topic of interest that everyone relates to 🥗. Explaining how we can better understand labels, easily compare products and make more informed food choices always generates a lot of interest and enthusiasm.

Presenting Open Food Facts is a piece of cake 😉🍰

We have ready-made LibreOffice, Powerpoint presentations you can translate and adapt into your language (if they've not yet been adapted). Também temos transcrições e vídeos de pessoas que apresentam o Open Food Facts em vários eventos.

Handling Q&A after your presentation 🔎

As pessoas costumam fazer muitas perguntas porque o projeto e sua apresentação foram instigantes. It's a good sign and nothing to be worried about:

  • Há uma página com as perguntas frequentes sobre o projeto. It lists those that we get asked the most often.
  • If you don't know the question (which happens to all of us, promise!🤭), it's always possible to say: "I'm not sure, but I think that…", "I don't have the answer right now, but I'd be happy to get in touch after the talk when I've found out!"
