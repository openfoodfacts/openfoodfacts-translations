Energetska ocena je pomembna izbira pri nakupu hladilnika. Hranilna vrednost bo kmalu lahko tudi za hrano.



Poenostavitev označevanja hranilne vrednosti na embalaži, ki potrošnikom pomaga izbrati bolj zdravo hrano, je cilj petkolornega sistema točkovanja, ki ga je predlagal profesor Serge Hercberg iz francoskega Nacionalnega programa prehrane in zdravja.



V istem duhu kot energetska oznaka naprave, ta sistem zagotavlja oceno od A do E (vsaka je povezana z barvo zelene do rdeče), ki bi bila prostovoljno prikazana na sprednji strani izdelkov. . Ta sistem je del zakona o zdravju Marisol Touraine, o katerem je bila razprava v Državnem zboru 31. marca 2015.


Več sadja in zelenjave, beljakovin in vlaknin, manj kalorij, sladkorja, nasičenih maščob in soli.

"5 sadij in zelenjave na dan" , "manj maščobe, manj sladko, manj slano" : barvne opombe poskušajo sintetizirati ta sporočila, ki so splošno znana javnosti, vendar niso nujno enostavna za uporabo pri nakupovanju.

Da bi se ugotovil prehrambeni rezultat, se najprej izračuna nutritivni rezultat, ki upošteva vsebino elementov, za katere je bilo ugotovljeno, da so hranilno ugodne (sadje in zelenjava, beljakovine in vlaknine) in elementi, ki se štejejo za neugodne, (energija, preprosti sladkorji, nasičene maščobne kisline in natrij, ki ustrezajo soli). Zelene, rumene, oranžne, vijolična in rdeče barve (A, B, C, D, E) ustrezajo različnim presledkom hranilne vrednosti.

Opomba za primerjavo živil pri nakupovanju

Unlike other rating systems (such as recommended daily intake percentages), the score is independent of the portion indicated by the manufacturer, which is often different for each brand and is sometimes underestimated compared to the portion actually consumed.
Not linking the note to the size of the portion makes it possible to compare the notes of different products before choosing which one to buy.

Barvne opombe v Open Food Facts

Konec septembra 2014 smo se obrnili na profesorja Hercberga, da bi ga vprašali za natančno formulo hranilne vrednosti in pragove za določanje 5-barvnih opomb A / B / C / D / E, priporočenih v njegovem poročilu "Predlogi za nov zagon prehranske politike javnega zdravja kot dela nacionalne zdravstvene strategije".

Ekipa profesorja Hercberga nam je poslala formulo za izračun in vse informacije, ki so potrebne za izračun rezultata in hranilne vrednosti, zato smo lahko uporabili formulo za izdelke v Open Food Facts skupne zbirke podatkov. Točkovanje je možno za izdelke, za katere je na embalaži prikazana dovolj popolna prehranska tabela. Do danes smo lahko določili oceno več kot 10.000 prehrambenih izdelkov.

Oglejte si prehranske opombe na vašem pametnem telefonu v razširjeni realnosti

We display nutritional score and color ratings on each Open Food Facts product sheet, and are also featured in our Android , iPhone and Windows Phone mobile apps .

While waiting for the possible adoption of the color note system, and its possible voluntary application by the manufacturers on the products, you can now scan the product barcodes with the Open Food Facts application to see the nutritional ratings.

On Android mobiles (and soon iPhone), you do not even need to scan the barcode: thanks to the visual recognition technology of the company Moodstocks , simply place your phone in front of a product to see its nutritional score , and that even without internet connection, which is very useful especially in stores.

Teža besed, šok podatkov

Beyond the word battles, the Open Food Facts association aims at transparency and openness of data on food products. We are convinced that these data are data of public interest - they are too important to be kept closed - and that is why we started 3 years ago to build a collaborative, free and open database , in which more than 1,500 contributors added data from more than 35,000 products.

These data have very numerous applications: decryption of labels (additives, allergens, etc.), comparison and research of products, education ... a dozen reuses of Open Food Facts data have already been inventoried.

This data can test, evaluate and refine a nutritional scoring system as a whole by applying it to 10,000 products rather than to weaker samples or focusing on emblematic but sometimes anecdotal examples.

To this end, we have put in place a series of tools so that everyone can test the system of color notes of the National Nutrition and Health Program:

Display of the distribution of nutritional notes

The Open Food Facts website allows you to explore the database in different facets such as product categories, brands, labels, and now nutritional notes. It is now very easy to obtain the distribution of nutritional notes for all products or for a given brand or category, for example breakfast cereals, and to display the corresponding products at a certain grade.


Nutritional notes of breakfast cereals - Source Open Food Facts

Nutrition notes by product category

It is interesting to compare the nutritional notes of the different categories of products. We can see the categories of the best rated products (fruits and vegetables, compotes) and the lowest rated (chocolate, sausage, mayonnaise).

You can also see which categories have products that have a wide variety of ratings. It is for these categories that the rating system is the most useful because it allows consumers to take into account the nutritional score in their choices, and it can encourage manufacturers to improve the formulation of their products for them get better grades.

On the other hand, the score is less useful for categories where all products have almost the same nutritional values ​​as honeys that all obtain the D score. The system can then compare to similar categories such as spreads pasta or jams for honey .

Nutritional notes of color in interactive charts

With a few clicks, the Open Food Facts search form allows you to create interactive charts that show all the products corresponding to certain criteria (for example category, brand, label ...) on 2 axes that you can choose (all nutrients, minerals, vitamins, number of additives, nutritional score etc.).
It is now possible to also show the nutritional grade of color.

From Citizen Data Collection to Citizen Science

We would like to thank the team of Professor Hercberg of the National Nutrition and Health Program who trusted us and gave us formulas for calculating nutritional scores and all the changes and adaptations made since September, and in particular Dr Chantal Julia who answered all our questions and requests for clarification.

We are also very pleased to have been able to provide Professor Hercberg's team with our entire product database enriched with nutritional score. Product databases such as Nutrinet and Open Food Facts allow you to test and refine the calculation formulas and the different thresholds so that the nutritional notes are the most useful.

Odpri dejstva o hrani is a citizen project from civil society (the entire project team and 1500 contributors are volunteers). We use crowdsourcing to gather food data from around the world. Projects like the Wikipedia encyclopedia and the OpenStreetMap collaborative and free map have shown that people around the world can come together to achieve great things. Wikipedia and OpenStreetMap are our models and we hope that Open Food Facts can also help improve the nutrition of everyone. We are therefore particularly happy to have had the opportunity to interact and collaborate with a scientific team. It is for us a first experience of citizen science that we intend to renew.
